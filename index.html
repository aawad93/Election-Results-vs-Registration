District Registration & Their Effect on Election Results

It is widely accepted that a district's partisan registration is a almost always predictive of the election results. However, this project explores the data behind this claim.

Looking at California's election results and voter registration data from 2006-2018, we can look at how redistricting and turnout affect results on all sizes of districts. In a theoretical world where every citizen votes according to their party identification and turnout 100%, election results and voter registration breakdown would be identical. However, that is far from the reality. With election's turnout fluctuating based on popular interest and redistricting every 10 years altering the shape of districts, how strong is the relationship between a district's partisan breakdown and its results?
